# Code of Conduct & Manifesto

This document has two parts:

1. **The Code of Conduct** — how we treat one another when participating in this project's spaces (issues, pull requests, discussions, related community channels).
2. **The Project Values** — what Mahalle is *for* and what it refuses to be. The full charter lives in [MANIFESTO.md](./MANIFESTO.md).

The Code of Conduct governs *people interacting with the project*. The Manifesto governs *what the project itself is*. Both apply.

---

## Part 1 — Contributor Code of Conduct

### Our Pledge

We — contributors, maintainers, and community members — pledge to make participation in this project a welcoming experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, visible or invisible disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socio-economic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, caste, color, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

We pledge to act and interact in ways that contribute to an open, welcoming, diverse, inclusive, and healthy community.

### Our Standards

Examples of behavior that contributes to a positive environment include:

- Demonstrating empathy and kindness toward other people
- Being respectful of differing opinions, viewpoints, and experiences
- Giving and gracefully accepting constructive feedback
- Accepting responsibility, apologizing to those affected by our mistakes, and learning from the experience
- Focusing on what is best not just for us as individuals, but for the overall community

Behavior that is **not acceptable** includes harassment of any kind, personal or political attacks, publishing others' private information without permission, and other conduct which could reasonably be considered inappropriate in a professional setting. The full enumeration of unacceptable behavior is defined by the Contributor Covenant 2.1, linked below.

### Enforcement Responsibilities

Project maintainers are responsible for clarifying and enforcing these standards. They will take appropriate and fair corrective action in response to any behavior they deem inappropriate, threatening, offensive, or harmful, and may remove, edit, or reject contributions that are not aligned with this Code of Conduct.

### Scope

This Code of Conduct applies in all project spaces — repository issues and pull requests, discussion forums, official chat channels, and any setting where an individual is representing the project or its community.

### Enforcement

Instances of unacceptable behavior may be reported to the project maintainers at:

**`atakee@gmail.com`**

All reports will be reviewed and investigated promptly and fairly. Maintainers are obligated to respect the privacy and safety of the reporter.

## Part 2 — Project Values

Mahalle is built around a set of non-negotiable values that go beyond contributor behavior. They describe **who the project is for, what it must remain, and what it refuses to become.**

These values are codified in [**MANIFESTO.md**](./MANIFESTO.md). In summary:

- Mahalle is software for **neighborhoods to organize themselves democratically** — not for audiences, markets, or constituencies.
- Mahalle is **not for political parties, commercial extraction, religious proselytizing, resident surveillance, or any single person's image or career**.
- Anyone deploying Mahalle is expected to maintain transparent governance, a public moderation log, transparent funding, and a community exit clause.
- The name "Mahalle" belongs to the project. Forks branded as "Mahalle" must honor the manifesto.

If you intend to contribute code, deploy the platform, or fork the project: **read [MANIFESTO.md](./MANIFESTO.md) in full**. If you cannot honor what is written there, please do not use this software.
