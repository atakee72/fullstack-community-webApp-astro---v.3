Every Tuesday and Friday, Schillerpromenade transforms into a bustling open-air market. Whether you're a seasoned regular or a first-timer, here's what you need to know.

## When and Where

The market runs from **9:00 to 14:00** on the central promenade. Arrive early for the best selection — the organic bread stall tends to sell out by 11.

## What You'll Find

### Fresh Produce
Local farmers bring seasonal fruits and vegetables directly from Brandenburg. In winter, expect root vegetables, kale, and stored apples. Summer brings berries, tomatoes, and fresh herbs.

### Specialty Vendors
- **Käse König** — Artisan cheeses from small German dairies
- **Oliven & Mehr** — Mediterranean olives, oils, and dips
- **Bäckerei Schmidt** — Sourdough and Vollkornbrot baked fresh that morning
- **Blumen Ecke** — Seasonal flowers and potted herbs

### Street Food
Grab a quick bite between shopping:
- Turkish gözleme made fresh on the griddle
- Vietnamese pho from the corner stall
- Classic Currywurst (of course)

## Tips from Regulars

1. **Bring your own bags** — most vendors don't provide plastic
2. **Cash is king** — a few stalls accept card, but don't count on it
3. **Chat with the vendors** — they love sharing recipes and tips
4. **Check the notice board** — community events are often posted at the market entrance

## Beyond the Market

After shopping, grab a coffee at one of the cafes along the promenade and watch the neighborhood go by. It's one of those small rituals that makes living here feel like home.

See you at the market!
