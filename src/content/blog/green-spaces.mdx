Everyone knows Tempelhofer Feld — the massive former airport turned public park. But Schillerkiez has plenty of smaller, quieter green spaces worth exploring.

## Schillerpromenade Gardens

The promenade itself is lined with mature plane trees that create a natural canopy in summer. Benches along the path are perfect for reading or people-watching.

## Community Gardens

### Allmende-Kontor
Located at the edge of Tempelhofer Feld, this community garden is a patchwork of individually maintained plots. It's a place where neighbors grow vegetables, herbs, and flowers side by side.

### Stadtgarten Schillerkiez
A newer initiative tucked behind the residential blocks. Open to anyone who wants to join — no experience needed.

## Pocket Parks

Small doesn't mean insignificant. These micro-parks offer a breather between the buildings:

- **Wartheplatz** — A small square with playground equipment and mature trees. Surprisingly quiet for its central location.
- **The courtyard gardens** — Many of the Altbau buildings have hidden Hinterhof gardens. If you live in one, you already know the magic. If you don't, befriend a neighbor.

## Why Green Spaces Matter

Studies consistently show that access to green spaces improves mental health, reduces stress, and strengthens community bonds. In a dense urban neighborhood like ours, every patch of green counts.

## Get Involved

The community gardens are always looking for new members. Check the [Forum](/) for upcoming planting days, or just show up with a pair of gloves and a willingness to get your hands dirty.
