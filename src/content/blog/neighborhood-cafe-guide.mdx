Schillerkiez runs on coffee. Whether you need a quick espresso on the way to the U-Bahn or a quiet corner to work for the afternoon, the neighborhood has you covered.

## The Classics

### Promenade Cafe
Right on Schillerpromenade, this is the neighborhood living room. Outdoor seating in summer, board games in winter, and a Milchkaffee that tastes like it's always tasted. Not fancy, but essential.

### Eckladen
Part cafe, part corner shop, part community hub. The kind of place where you come in for a coffee and leave two hours later having learned about three neighborhood events and someone's grandmother's recipe for Apfelkuchen.

## The Specialty Spots

### Barn Roasters Pop-Up
Berlin's specialty coffee scene occasionally pops up in the Kiez. Follow the community board for dates — these events tend to draw a crowd.

### The Home Barista
One of our neighbors runs a small roasting operation from their apartment and sells bags at the weekly market. Ask for the Ethiopian single-origin.

## Unwritten Rules

1. **Don't camp for hours on a single espresso** — order something every couple of hours if you're working
2. **Tip in cash** — most baristas prefer it
3. **Say hello** — a "Moin" or "Hallo" goes a long way
4. **Try the cake** — every cafe has a signature Kuchen. Ask which one.
