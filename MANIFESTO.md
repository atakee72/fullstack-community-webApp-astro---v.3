# The Mahalle Manifesto

> *Mahalle* (Turkish: neighborhood) — a place where people who happen to live near each other become a community by talking, listening, and deciding together.

This document is the moral charter of the Mahalle project. It describes who Mahalle is for, what it must remain, and what it refuses to become. It is referenced by the [`LICENSE`](./LICENSE) and is intended to bind anyone who deploys, forks, or contributes to this project — not by force of law, but by the stronger force of a shared intent made explicit.

If you cannot honor what is written here, please do not use this software.

---

## What Mahalle is for

Mahalle is software for **local neighborhoods to organize themselves democratically**. It exists so that residents of a place can:

- See and speak to each other across the languages, cultures, and generations that share their street.
- Make decisions about their shared spaces, events, and concerns through visible, accountable processes.
- Hold representatives — building reps, neighborhood councils, local officials — to public account.
- Practice the small civic muscles that hold a society together: showing up, listening, disagreeing without contempt, and trying again tomorrow.

Mahalle treats neighborhoods as communities, not as audiences, markets, or constituencies.

## Who Mahalle is for

Mahalle is built to be deployed and operated by communities that represent more than themselves. In practice, this means:

- **Registered neighborhood associations** (Verein, dernek, association loi 1901, or equivalent under local law).
- **Housing cooperatives, community land trusts, and tenant unions.**
- **Public institutions** acting on behalf of residents — municipalities, district councils, Bezirksämter.
- **Informal community groups** with open membership, transparent decision-making, and no single owner.
- **Researchers, educators, and civic-tech contributors** working on the project itself.

Mahalle is **not** for individual residents to deploy as their personal community. A community of one is not a community.

## What Mahalle refuses to be

Mahalle will not be operated by, branded as, or used in service of:

- **Political parties or partisan campaigns.** A neighborhood is not a voter base. The platform must remain a place where people of different political views can meet without their participation becoming a party asset.
- **For-profit commercial extraction.** Resident attention, conversation, and data are not products. Advertising, lead generation, "growth hacking," and surveillance economics are incompatible with the mission. (Codified in the PolyForm Noncommercial license.)
- **Religious proselytizing or sectarian organizing.** Faith communities are welcome to use Mahalle to coordinate within themselves, but the platform itself stays neutral and shared.
- **Surveillance of residents** by landlords, employers, governments, or anyone else. No covert data collection, no behavioral profiling sold downstream, no "analytics" that strip away the privacy of the people who showed up to talk to their neighbors.
- **A vehicle for any single person's image, agenda, or career**, including the original author's. Mahalle outgrows the founder; that is the point.

## How Mahalle stays democratic

Anyone deploying Mahalle for a community is expected — not legally compelled, but expected — to maintain at minimum:

- **A governance body of at least three community members**, drawn from the user base, with authority over moderation policy and platform changes that affect users.
- **A public moderation log** so that decisions about removing content, banning users, or escalating reports are visible to the community they serve.
- **A transparent funding source** — if Mahalle costs money to run, where the money comes from is public.
- **An exit clause** — if the operator becomes unable or unwilling to continue, the community can take over the data and the deployment without paying ransom.

These are not bureaucratic requirements; they are the difference between a *community platform* and a *community-shaped private fiefdom*.

## On the name

The word *Mahalle* and the project name belong to the project, not to its current operators. The author retains trademark rights specifically to prevent the name from being captured by political movements, commercial actors, or single individuals. Forks of the code are welcome under the [LICENSE](./LICENSE); forks branded as "Mahalle" are not, unless they honor this manifesto.

## On the founder

The original author of Mahalle (Ercan Atak) intends, when the project matures, to transfer the trademark and stewardship of the project to a community-governed nonprofit entity, so that no single person — including him — has unilateral control over what Mahalle becomes. Until that transfer happens, this manifesto is the binding statement of intent.
